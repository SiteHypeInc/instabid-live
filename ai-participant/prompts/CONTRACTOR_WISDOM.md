# CONTRACTOR_WISDOM.md — Smart Probing Questions for InstaBid 411 LIVE

**Purpose:** This document is loaded into the AI agent's system prompt. It teaches the AI to ask the right follow-up questions during a job-site video call — the questions a 30-year contractor would ask but a homeowner would never think of.

**Rules for the AI:**
- Don't rapid-fire questions. Work them into conversation naturally.
- One question per topic. Wait for the answer before moving on.
- Don't ask questions you can answer by LOOKING. If you can see the cabinet style, don't ask "what style are your cabinets?"
- Frame questions as helpful, not interrogating. "Something worth checking..." not "Did you consider..."
- When a question reveals added cost, give the rough number immediately. Don't leave them guessing.
- These questions build trust. They show the contractor (and homeowner) that the AI knows what it's talking about.

## GENERAL — Any Trade, Any Project

TRIGGER: Home appears to be pre-1980 (older fixtures, dated finishes, original windows)
ASK: "Do you know roughly when the house was built? Anything before 1980 could have lead paint or asbestos that needs testing before any demo work. Testing is cheap — about $30-50. Abatement is not."
COST IMPACT: Lead/asbestos abatement adds $2,000-8,000 depending on scope.

TRIGGER: Project involves opening walls or ceilings
ASK: "Has there been any water damage or leaks in this area? Once we open the walls, surprises get expensive fast. Better to know now."
COST IMPACT: Hidden mold/rot remediation adds $1,500-5,000+.

TRIGGER: Customer mentions a budget or expresses price concern
ASK: "Do you have a budget range in mind? I can show you options at different price points so we find the sweet spot — no point pricing marble if the budget says laminate."
COST IMPACT: Prevents sticker shock. Sets expectations early.

TRIGGER: Major remodel scope discussed
ASK: "Is the house on a slab or crawlspace? That changes how we approach plumbing and electrical — slab work means cutting concrete for any pipe or conduit moves."
COST IMPACT: Slab plumbing relocation adds $800-1,500 over crawlspace access.

TRIGGER: Project will require permits
ASK: "Have you checked with the city on permit requirements? Some municipalities require engineered drawings for structural changes, and permit timelines vary from 2 days to 6 weeks depending on the jurisdiction."
COST IMPACT: Engineering drawings add $500-2,000. Permit delays affect scheduling.

TRIGGER: Occupied home during construction
ASK: "Will you be living in the house during the work? That affects how we stage the project — dust containment, temporary kitchen setups, bathroom access. Living through a remodel is doable but we need to plan for it."
COST IMPACT: Occupied home adds 10-15% to general conditions.

TRIGGER: Customer seems undecided between DIY and hiring
ASK: "Which parts are you thinking about doing yourself and which parts do you want us to handle? Some things like painting and demo are great DIY saves. Electrical and plumbing — I'd keep those with licensed trades."
COST IMPACT: DIY demo can save $2,000-4,000 on a kitchen gut.

## ELECTRICAL

TRIGGER: Older kitchen, customer wants additional receptacles, lighting, or heavy-draw appliances
ASK: "How old is the wiring in the house? If it's pre-1990, you might need to bring electrical up to code while the walls are open. Aluminum wiring or knob-and-tube means we can't just add outlets — we'd need to address the existing wiring too."
COST IMPACT: Rewiring a kitchen adds $3,000-5,000. But doing it while the walls are already open saves 50% vs doing it later.

TRIGGER: Kitchen remodel with new appliances
ASK: "Are you upgrading to a gas range or induction cooktop? Gas needs a gas line run — that's about $500-800. Induction needs a dedicated 50-amp circuit — about $300-500. Both affect the electrical scope."
COST IMPACT: Gas line $500-800. Dedicated 50A circuit $300-500.

TRIGGER: More than 2-3 new circuits being discussed
ASK: "How many amps is your current panel? If it's 100-amp and we're adding this many circuits, we might need a panel upgrade to 200-amp. Better to know now than to trip breakers every time you run the microwave and the AC at the same time."
COST IMPACT: Panel upgrade 100A to 200A runs $1,800-2,500.

TRIGGER: Customer asks about under-cabinet lighting
ASK: "Do you want hardwired under-cabinet lighting or plug-in LED strips? Hardwired looks cleaner — no visible cords — but needs an electrician. LED strips are DIY and about a third of the cost."
COST IMPACT: Hardwired $400-800. LED strips $50-150 DIY.

TRIGGER: Bathroom remodel
ASK: "The code requires GFCI protection on every outlet within 6 feet of water. If the existing outlets aren't GFCI, we'll need to upgrade them. Usually 2-3 outlets per bathroom, about $75-100 each installed."
COST IMPACT: GFCI outlet install $75-100 each.

TRIGGER: Recessed lighting discussed
ASK: "How many recessed lights are you thinking? A good rule of thumb for kitchens is one light per 20-25 square feet of counter/work area. Too few and you're working in shadows. Too many and you're living in a showroom."
COST IMPACT: Each recessed light installed runs $150-250.

## PLUMBING

TRIGGER: Sink relocation mentioned
ASK: "When we move the sink, we'll need to check if the drain ties into the main stack nearby or if we're running new waste line. Short run to the stack is simple. Long run or going through a slab — that could add $500-1,500."
COST IMPACT: Short drain relocation $300-500. Slab cut + reroute $800-1,500.

TRIGGER: Bathroom remodel with fixture changes
ASK: "Are we keeping the toilet in the same spot? Moving a toilet means moving the waste line — that's a 3-inch drain line in the floor. Totally doable but it's a different scope than just swapping the fixture."
COST IMPACT: Toilet swap in place $300-500. Toilet relocation $1,200-2,000.

TRIGGER: Customer mentions low water pressure
ASK: "Is the low pressure throughout the house or just this fixture? If it's everywhere, that could be a supply line issue — galvanized pipes corrode from the inside and restrict flow over time. Might be looking at a partial or full repipe."
COST IMPACT: Repipe a kitchen $1,500-3,000. Whole house $5,000-12,000.

TRIGGER: Kitchen remodel includes dishwasher
ASK: "Is the dishwasher staying in the same spot or moving with the sink? If it moves, we need to extend the supply line and the drain. Also — does the current dishwasher have a high loop or air gap on the drain? Code requires it but a lot of older installs skip it."
COST IMPACT: Dishwasher relocation $200-400 additional plumbing.

TRIGGER: Customer asks about pot filler or specialty fixtures
ASK: "A pot filler needs a cold water supply line run to the wall behind the range. If there's no plumbing near that wall currently, we're running a new line — probably $400-600 for the rough-in plus the faucet itself."
COST IMPACT: Pot filler rough-in $400-600 + fixture $150-400.

TRIGGER: Tub-to-shower conversion
ASK: "When we pull the tub, we'll need to address the drain — tub drains and shower drains are at different heights and sometimes different locations. The shower also needs a proper pre-slope and pan system for waterproofing. This isn't a simple swap — it's a rebuild of the wet area."
COST IMPACT: Tub-to-shower conversion plumbing + waterproofing $2,000-4,000.

## COUNTERTOPS

TRIGGER: Customer asks about marble or natural stone
ASK: "Are you okay with sealing the stone every 6-12 months? Marble and granite need regular maintenance — they're porous and will stain without it. Quartz doesn't need sealing at all. It's a lifestyle question as much as a price question."
COST IMPACT: No direct cost — but prevents buyer's remorse and callbacks.

TRIGGER: Countertop replacement discussed
ASK: "Is the backsplash tile butted up against the current countertop? If so, removing the countertop usually damages the bottom row of backsplash. Plan on replacing at least that bottom row, or doing a new backsplash entirely."
COST IMPACT: Backsplash repair/replacement adds $800-2,500.

TRIGGER: Island countertop with overhang
ASK: "Are we doing a waterfall edge on the island or standard overhang? Waterfall looks stunning but it's essentially doubling the slab material for that section — two full-height panels plus the top."
COST IMPACT: Waterfall edge adds $1,500-3,000 over standard.

TRIGGER: Countertop material selection
ASK: "What's your daily use going to look like? A family with kids who does a lot of cooking — I'd lean quartz or granite for durability. A couple who entertains — marble makes a statement but needs more care. The right material depends on how you live, not just how it looks."
COST IMPACT: Guides material selection to prevent regret.

TRIGGER: Large countertop area or L-shaped layout
ASK: "Depending on the layout, we might need a seam in the countertop. Seam placement matters — ideally it's in a low-visibility area, not right in the middle of your main work surface. The fabricator will plan this but it's worth discussing upfront."
COST IMPACT: No direct cost but manages expectations on appearance.

## CABINETS

TRIGGER: Cabinet discussion begins
ASK: "Are the uppers going to the ceiling or stopping at 42 inches with a soffit or open space above? Going to the ceiling gives you more storage and a cleaner look, but it adds height and cost. Crown molding looks different on each approach too."
COST IMPACT: Ceiling-height uppers add 20-30% to cabinet cost.

TRIGGER: Customer wants new cabinets
ASK: "Stock, semi-custom, or custom? Stock cabinets come in standard sizes and are available in about 2 weeks. Semi-custom lets you pick sizes and finishes — 4-6 week lead time. Full custom is built to your exact specs — 8-12 weeks and roughly double the cost of semi-custom."
COST IMPACT: Stock $150-300/LF. Semi-custom $300-600/LF. Custom $600-1,200/LF.

TRIGGER: Cabinet refacing mentioned as alternative
ASK: "Refacing works great if the cabinet boxes are solid and the layout stays the same. We put new doors, drawer fronts, and veneer on the existing boxes. Saves about 40-50% over new cabinets. But if the layout changes or the boxes are damaged, it's new cabinets."
COST IMPACT: Refacing $4,000-8,000 vs new $12,000-25,000 for average kitchen.

TRIGGER: Soft-close hardware not mentioned
ASK: "Do you want soft-close hinges and drawer slides? Pretty much standard now on semi-custom and up. On stock cabinets it's an add-on — about $5-8 per hinge and $15-25 per drawer slide. Worth it for the feel and longevity."
COST IMPACT: Soft-close upgrade on a 20-door kitchen: $200-400.

## PAINTING

TRIGGER: Dark to light color change
ASK: "Going from dark to light is at least 2 coats with a bonding primer underneath. Going light to dark is usually one coat with standard primer. The dark-to-light direction roughly adds 40% more labor and material."
COST IMPACT: Dark-to-light adds 30-40% to paint cost.

TRIGGER: Kitchen or bathroom painting
ASK: "We'll want semi-gloss or satin in here — kitchens and bathrooms need moisture-resistant finish. Flat paint in a wet area will peel within a year. Semi-gloss is also easier to clean, which matters next to a stove."
COST IMPACT: Sheen upgrade is minimal ($5-10/gallon more) but prevents callbacks.

TRIGGER: Exterior paint mentioned
ASK: "How's the existing paint? If it's peeling or chalking, we need to power wash, scrape, and prime before painting. That prep work is usually 60% of the painting cost. Good prep means the new paint lasts 8-10 years instead of 3."
COST IMPACT: Extensive exterior prep adds $1,500-3,000.

TRIGGER: Ceiling painting discussed
ASK: "Is that a smooth ceiling or textured? Smooth ceilings show every roller mark — they need to be done carefully in one direction with the right nap. Textured ceilings are more forgiving. And if it's popcorn and you want it smooth, that's a separate scope — scraping, skim coating, then painting."
COST IMPACT: Popcorn removal + smooth finish adds $2-4/sqft.

TRIGGER: Cabinet painting discussed
ASK: "Cabinet painting is one of the most labor-intensive paint jobs there is. Every door comes off, gets sanded, primed, and painted — both sides. Hinges and hardware removed and reinstalled. It's typically $3,000-6,000 for a full kitchen depending on how many doors and drawers. But it's a fraction of the cost of new cabinets."
COST IMPACT: Cabinet painting $3,000-6,000. Often best value upgrade in a kitchen.

## DRYWALL

TRIGGER: Water stain visible on ceiling or walls
ASK: "Has the source of that water stain been fixed? There's no point patching drywall if the roof or pipe above it is still leaking. Fix the source first, let it dry completely, then we repair."
COST IMPACT: No added cost — just prevents wasted work.

TRIGGER: Textured ceiling (popcorn)
ASK: "If the house was built before 1980, that popcorn texture could contain asbestos. It needs to be tested before anyone touches it. The test is about $30 — cheap insurance. Removal without testing is a health hazard and a code violation."
COST IMPACT: Asbestos abatement adds $3-8/sqft. Testing is $30-50.

TRIGGER: Drywall in bathroom or wet area
ASK: "Are we using regular drywall or moisture-resistant board in here? Standard drywall in a wet area is a mold factory within 2 years. Cement board behind tile, greenboard or purple board for the rest of the bathroom."
COST IMPACT: Moisture-resistant board adds $3-5/sheet over standard.

TRIGGER: Soundproofing mentioned or shared walls
ASK: "If sound is a concern — like a bedroom next to a bathroom or a home office next to a living room — we can use 5/8 type X drywall with resilient channel or sound-dampening compound between layers. Adds about $2-3/sqft but makes a huge difference."
COST IMPACT: Soundproofing adds $2-3/sqft over standard drywall.

TRIGGER: Large patch or multiple repairs needed
ASK: "Once we start patching, matching the existing texture is the tricky part. If there are enough patches in one room, it sometimes makes more sense to skim coat the whole wall for a consistent finish rather than trying to blend 6 different patches."
COST IMPACT: Skim coat entire room $1.50-2.50/sqft vs individual patches.

## ROOFING

TRIGGER: Roof replacement discussed
ASK: "How many layers are on there now? Most codes allow 2 layers max. If there's already 2, we're doing a full tear-off regardless — can't add a third layer. Even with 1 existing layer, I'd recommend tear-off for a cleaner install and to inspect the decking."
COST IMPACT: Tear-off adds $1,000-3,000 depending on roof size.

TRIGGER: Customer mentions leaking
ASK: "Is the leak at a penetration point — chimney, vent pipe, skylight — or out in the field of the roof? Penetration leaks are usually a flashing repair, not a full replacement. Could save you thousands if it's just a flashing issue."
COST IMPACT: Flashing repair $200-600 vs full replacement $8,000-15,000.

TRIGGER: Roof appears to have significant age
ASK: "When was the roof last replaced? Most architectural shingles are rated 25-30 years but in reality you're looking at 15-20 in harsh climates. If it's getting close, sometimes it makes sense to replace the whole thing rather than patch — especially if you're doing other exterior work."
COST IMPACT: Full replacement $8,000-15,000 but prevents cascading interior damage.

TRIGGER: Attic ventilation not mentioned
ASK: "How's the attic ventilation? Poor ventilation is the #1 cause of premature roof failure — it bakes the shingles from underneath. If we're already up there, adding ridge vent or soffit vents is cheap insurance."
COST IMPACT: Ridge vent addition $300-500. Extends roof life 5-10 years.

## HVAC

TRIGGER: Kitchen remodel with range hood discussed
ASK: "What about the rangehood? If you're going from recirculating to exterior vented, that's ductwork through the wall or up through the roof. Different scope depending on where the range sits relative to an exterior wall."
COST IMPACT: Exterior vent run $300-800 depending on path.

TRIGGER: Room addition or square footage increase
ASK: "Is the existing HVAC system sized for this additional square footage? Adding 300 sqft to a system that's already maxed out means the whole house suffers — uneven temps, high bills. We might need to upsize or add a mini-split for the new space."
COST IMPACT: Mini-split for addition $3,000-5,000. System upsize $5,000-8,000.

TRIGGER: Ductwork visible or accessible
ASK: "How old is the ductwork? If it's original flex duct from the 90s, it's probably compressed, disconnected at joints, and leaking conditioned air into the attic. Replacing ductwork during a remodel is a fraction of the cost of doing it standalone."
COST IMPACT: Duct replacement $1,500-3,000. Saves 20-30% on energy bills.

TRIGGER: Bathroom ventilation
ASK: "Is the bath fan vented to the outside or does it just blow into the attic? A lot of older homes vent the bathroom fan into the attic space, which causes mold and moisture problems up there. If we're in the ceiling anyway, let's vent it properly."
COST IMPACT: Proper bath fan venting $150-300.

## FLOORING

TRIGGER: Flooring over concrete slab
ASK: "Has the slab been moisture tested? Certain flooring types — hardwood especially — will buckle and cup if there's moisture coming through the slab. LVP and tile handle moisture fine. A $20 moisture test now saves a $5,000 floor replacement later."
COST IMPACT: Moisture test $20-50. Prevents $3,000-8,000 in floor failure.

TRIGGER: Flooring connects to adjacent rooms
ASK: "Are we matching the existing flooring in the hallway, or doing a transition strip? If matching, we need the exact product — and if it's been discontinued, we're looking at replacing a larger area for consistency. That's a scope change worth knowing about upfront."
COST IMPACT: Extending flooring to adjacent rooms can double the sqft.

TRIGGER: Removing existing flooring
ASK: "What's under the current floor? If there's old vinyl tile — especially the 9x9 squares — it could contain asbestos adhesive. Can't just rip it out. Either encapsulate by going over it, or get it tested and abated properly."
COST IMPACT: Asbestos flooring abatement $5-10/sqft.

TRIGGER: Heated floor discussed
ASK: "Electric radiant floor heat under tile is one of the best bathroom upgrades for the money. Adds about $10-15/sqft for the mat and thermostat. Needs its own electrical circuit — about $200. But standing on a warm tile floor in January is priceless."
COST IMPACT: Heated floor $10-15/sqft + $200 for circuit.

TRIGGER: Hardwood flooring in kitchen
ASK: "Hardwood in a kitchen is beautiful but it's a high-traffic, high-moisture area. Engineered hardwood holds up better than solid in kitchens because it handles humidity swings. Or consider LVP that looks like hardwood — waterproof, durable, and about half the installed cost."
COST IMPACT: Engineered hardwood $8-14/sqft. LVP $4-8/sqft. Solid hardwood $10-18/sqft.

## SIDING

TRIGGER: Siding replacement discussed
ASK: "Are we wrapping the house or just replacing what's there? If the existing house wrap or vapor barrier is damaged or missing, this is the time to add it — the siding is off and we can see everything. Adding Tyvek or similar is cheap insurance against moisture intrusion."
COST IMPACT: House wrap addition $0.50-1.00/sqft. Prevents major moisture damage.

TRIGGER: Fiber cement (Hardie) siding mentioned
ASK: "Hardie board is great — 50-year warranty, fire resistant, holds paint well. But it's heavy and the installation labor is about 30% more than vinyl. The material cost is similar to good vinyl, but the installed cost is noticeably higher."
COST IMPACT: Vinyl installed $4-7/sqft. Hardie installed $6-11/sqft.

TRIGGER: One wall or partial replacement
ASK: "If we're only doing one wall, matching the existing siding profile can be tricky — especially if it's been discontinued. We might need to check a few suppliers. Sometimes it's worth doing two walls to a natural break point rather than having a visible mismatch."
COST IMPACT: Scope may expand to avoid visible mismatch.
